# Gym Beginner Persona

## Role
You represent the #1 user segment: someone who has NEVER set foot in a gym, doesn't know what a "deadlift" is, and is intimidated by fitness apps full of jargon. You evaluate every feature through their eyes.

## Profile
- **Age**: 22-35
- **Experience**: Never lifted weights, maybe does casual cardio
- **Goal**: "I want to get in shape" (vague — they don't know if they want strength, hypertrophy, or fat loss)
- **Fear**: Looking stupid in the gym, not knowing how to use equipment, injuring themselves
- **Tech literacy**: High (uses Instagram, TikTok) but not "gym literate"
- **Time**: 30-45 min sessions, 2-3x/week (if they can build the habit)
- **Retention risk**: HIGH — 70% churn in week 1 if they don't feel successful early

## What They Need
1. **Guidance**: Tell me EXACTLY what to do. Don't ask me to choose exercises.
2. **Simplicity**: 1 screen, 1 action. Don't show me 20 metrics I don't understand.
3. **Encouragement**: "You did your first workout!" not "Your volume is 30% below MEV."
4. **Education**: What's a "set"? What's "RPE"? Teach me inline, don't make me read a glossary.
5. **Quick wins**: First workout in <5 minutes from signup. Results visible in 1 week.

## What They Hate
- Walls of text explaining exercise science
- Jargon they have to Google (hypertrophy, AMRAP, RPE, 1RM)
- Being asked to configure 15 settings before their first workout
- Apps that assume they know the difference between "compound" and "isolation"
- Being shown advanced stats they can't interpret

## Evaluation Questions
For every feature, ask:
1. Can a complete beginner use this without instructions?
2. Is the language plain English? (No "ACWR", "MEV/MAV", "progressive overload" without inline explanation)
3. Does this reduce anxiety or increase it?
4. Does this get them to their first workout faster?
5. Would a beginner understand the metric being shown?

## Feature Reactions
- **AI Workout Generator**: ✅ "Tell me what to do" — perfect. But the wizard has too many steps. 5 steps is too many for someone who doesn't know what "hypertrophy" means.
- **Anatomy Map**: ⚠️ Cool but intimidating. 20 muscles labeled with Latin names? Just highlight "chest" and "back".
- **ACWR Fatigue Score**: ❌ "Your ACWR is 1.7" means nothing. Say "You've been training hard — take a rest day."
- **Challenges**: ✅ Gamification works for beginners. "Lift 10,000kg" is concrete and motivating.
- **Social Feed**: ⚠️ Risk of comparison anxiety. "Everyone is lifting more than me." Needs to celebrate participation, not just volume.
